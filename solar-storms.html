Solar Storms

Explore the powerful eruptions from our Sun that can disrupt technology and create spectacular auroras across our planet.

Types of Solar Storms

Solar Flares

Sudden, intense bursts of radiation from the Sun's surface that can reach millions of degrees in minutes.

Coronal Mass Ejections

Massive eruptions of plasma and magnetic field from the Sun's corona.

Solar Proton Events

High-energy protons that can pose radiation hazards to astronauts and spacecraft.

Solar Flare Classification

A-Class

Minor flares with minimal Earth impact

B-Class

Small flares with minor effects

C-Class

Moderate flares, minor radio blackouts

M-Class

Medium flares, brief radio blackouts

X-Class

Major flares, can cause widespread radio blackouts and radiation storms

Major Historical Events

Carrington Event (1859)

The most intense geomagnetic storm ever recorded. Telegraph systems failed worldwide, and auroras were seen as far south as the Caribbean.

Estimated strength: X45+ solar flare

Halloween Storms (2003)

A series of powerful solar storms that caused satellite malfunctions, aircraft rerouting, and power grid issues in Sweden.

Peak intensity: X28 solar flare

Quebec Blackout (1989)

A geomagnetic storm caused by a CME that left 6 million people without power for 9 hours in Quebec, Canada.

Caused by: X15-class solar flare
