Space Weather Satellites

Discover the advanced technology monitoring our Sun and protecting Earth from solar storms and space weather events.

Key Space Weather Observatories

SOHO

Solar and Heliospheric Observatory - Studies the Sun's internal structure, outer atmosphere, and solar wind.

Launched: 1995 | Orbit: L1 Lagrange Point

SDO

Solar Dynamics Observatory - Provides ultra-high resolution images of the Sun in multiple wavelengths.

Launched: 2010 | Orbit: Geosynchronous

DSCOVR

Deep Space Climate Observatory - Monitors solar wind conditions and provides early warnings of geomagnetic storms.

Launched: 2015 | Orbit: L1 Lagrange Point

Advanced Monitoring Technology

Solar Imaging Instruments

  • Extreme Ultraviolet Variability Experiment (EVE)
  • Atmospheric Imaging Assembly (AIA)
  • Helioseismic and Magnetic Imager (HMI)
  • Large Angle Spectrometric Coronagraph (LASCO)

Particle Detectors

  • Solar Wind Electron Proton Alpha Monitor (SWEPAM)
  • Magnetospheric Multiscale (MMS) instruments
  • Cosmic Ray Isotope Spectrometer (CRIS)
  • Radiation Belt Storm Probes (RBSP)

Future Space Weather Missions

Parker Solar Probe

NASA's mission to "touch the Sun" - studying the solar corona and solar wind up close.

Closest approach: 3.83 million miles from Sun's surface

Solar Orbiter

ESA/NASA collaboration to study the Sun's poles and provide new insights into solar activity.

First images of Sun's poles: 2025
