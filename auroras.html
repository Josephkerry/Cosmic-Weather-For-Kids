The Magic of Auroras


Witness the breathtaking dance of light in Earth's polar skies, created by the Sun's charged particles meeting our atmosphere.

What Are Auroras?

Auroras, also known as northern lights (aurora borealis) and southern lights (aurora australis), are natural light displays in Earth's sky, predominantly seen in high-latitude regions.

They occur when charged particles from the Sun collide with atoms and molecules in Earth's atmosphere, exciting them and causing them to emit light of various colors.

Colors of the Northern Lights

Green

Most common color, caused by oxygen molecules about 60 miles above Earth. The most frequently seen aurora color.

Purple & Violet

Caused by nitrogen molecules, typically seen at lower altitudes during intense solar activity.

Red

Rare color caused by high-altitude oxygen, visible above 150 miles. Often appears as a faint red glow.

Best Places to Witness Auroras

Aurora Borealis (Northern Lights)

  • Iceland
  • Norway
  • Sweden & Finland
  • Canada (Yukon, Northwest Territories)
  • Alaska, USA

Aurora Australis (Southern Lights)

  • Tasmania, Australia
  • New Zealand (South Island)
  • Antarctica
  • Southern Argentina & Chile
  • Falkland Islands
